Program Structure
Peaks Coaching Group periodization
Your gym work follows the PCG 12-week model — two distinct routines (SW = Strength Workout, CBP = Core/Balance/Plyometrics) that evolve through 5 training phases.

📋 Two routines per gym day

  • SW — Strength Workout
    Bench press, lat pulldown, seated row, tricep pulldown, bicep curl, leg press, leg extension, leg curl, calf raise, back extension, ab curl, leg tucked rotation
  • CBP — Core / Balance / Plyometrics
    Slow side kicks, Bulgarian squat, knee balance, ball row, two-point push-up, Turkish get-ups, split squat jumps, lateral box push-off, lateral jumps

📈 12-week periodization (PCG)

  • Wk 1–2 Endurance / Adaptation60–70% · 20 reps · 3 sets
  • Wk 3, 6 Hypertrophy80% · 8–10 reps · 4–5 sets
  • Wk 4, 7–8 Strength95% · 3–6 reps · 4–6 sets
  • Wk 9 Power99% · 2–4 reps · 3–4 sets
  • Wk 5, 10–12 Endurance / Recovery70% · 15–20 reps · 2–4 sets
Coach note — GLP-1 & the PCG plan: The PCG program was designed for off-season cyclists. It aligns well with your situation — the early endurance/adaptation phase (Wks 1–2) matches your GLP-1 ramp-up period when you shouldn't yet be pushing hard. The strength phases (Wks 4, 7–8) are critical for muscle preservation as appetite suppression deepens. Log every session so we can catch strength decline early.

🚴 Cycling integration

  • Tuesday — Zone 2 steady, 60–75 min
  • Thursday — Steady (Wks 1–4) / Intervals (Wks 5+)
  • Saturday — Long ride, 90–120 min

🏋️ Gym days

  • Monday — SW upper + CBP (Wks 3+)
  • Wednesday — Rest/Mobility (Wks 1–4) / Full gym (Wks 5+)
  • Friday — SW lower + CBP
